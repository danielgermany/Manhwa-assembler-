A woman ran up to a policeman parked on the street, panic written all over her face.

"Officer, please! There's someone getting jumped in the alley over there!"

The cop gave her a calm, reassuring smile. "Don't worry, ma'am. We'll head over and check it out right away."

Relieved, the woman thanked him and walked off. The moment she was out of earshot, the female officer in the passenger seat turned to her partner.

"So… are we actually going to check it out?"

The male officer scoffed. "Are you serious? Did you see how many of them there were? Two cops versus a whole gang? That's not a rescue mission, that's a funeral with extra paperwork. Forget it. Let's just drive off before someone notices we're still here."

Real heroes, these two.

But just as they were about to pull away, a sharp voice cut through the alley.

"Hey, trash. Let him go. Right now."

The thugs surrounding their victim turned around, more annoyed than threatened.

"Huh? Who the hell are you? Mind your own business before you get hurt."

The two cops in the car froze, staring at the stranger like he'd just signed his own death certificate.

The man stepped forward, completely unbothered. "I'll only say it one more time. Let him go."

"Oh yeah? And what are you gonna—"

He never finished the sentence. The stranger closed the distance in a blink and slammed a clean uppercut straight into the lead thug's jaw. The guy dropped like someone cut his strings.

Everyone froze. Then the stranger pulled out a badge.

He was a cop. An off-duty one, apparently with a low tolerance for nonsense.

"I warned you. All of you, come at me. Let's get this over with."

The head thug spat blood and roared, "Beat this lunatic to death!"

What followed wasn't a fight. It was a demolition. The cop moved through them like they were standing still — every punch, every strike landing exactly where it needed to.

"Is this really all you've got?" he asked, almost bored. "Come on. Show me something."

Desperate, one of the thugs whipped out a knife.

The cop didn't even flinch. He just smiled.

"From the way you're holding that, I can tell you've never actually used it. Your eyes are giving you away. So go ahead — try to finish me. But if you can't…" he cracked his neck, "…it's my turn."

The thug's hands started shaking. The knife clattered to the ground.

"W-who… who are you?"

The cop sighed. "Relax. I'm just an ordinary police officer."

Just an ordinary officer. Sure. And I'm just an ordinary guy who definitely doesn't watch manhwa recaps at three in the morning.

---

**Scene shifts to a shipping yard.**

Stacks of cargo containers loomed under the night sky. One container creaked open, and a group of armed men stepped out, weapons drawn.

A voice from the shadows said calmly, "So everyone's here. Good."

"It's him! It's RMC's Shin Bang! You finally showed up."

Another voice cut in — colder, sharper. "Maple Leaf Shin. You took your time. You look way too relaxed for someone on his last mission."

Bang shrugged. "I'm not late. So what's the job?"

"You'll find out soon enough. Try to be patient."

"Yeah, I'd love to. Patience is just so easy when I'm surrounded by guys pointing guns at me."

One of them lunged at Bang, trying to chain his arms behind his back. Bang barely turned his head — just an elbow to the throat and the man crumpled.

Maple Leaf chuckled. "So you already figured it out."

"Wasn't hard. You called this my last mission. The 'inaugural retirement mission of the assassin Bang.' Real subtle naming. Whoever came up with that should be retired first."

Then the rest of them attacked.

What happened next was brutal. Bang moved through the entire group like a ghost — quick, surgical, and absolutely lethal. One by one they dropped, and when only the last one was left, Bang ended it without hesitation.

The yard fell silent.

He turned to the last surviving member, the one wheezing on the ground.

"Now talk. What does the Buddha want? Why is he doing this?"

The man coughed up blood, defiant. "I didn't want to use this card so early… but my opponent really is the legendary Bang. Damn it."

"Won't talk? Fine. I don't waste words on dead men."

A single gunshot rang out.

---

**Scene shifts again — this time to a temple.**

Incense smoke curled toward the ceiling. A monk sat cross-legged in prayer, his face calm, peaceful. Then his phone rang.

Because nothing kills a spiritual moment like a ringtone.

The monk picked up. "Yes?"

"Maple Leaf. Did you complete the mission?"

"Sir, every mission you sent has been a complete failure. Whoever's running your operation is a clown."

"…Yuan Gang. So it's you."

"Cut the act, Buddha. I know exactly where you are. You're in the temple right now, aren't you?"

The monk smiled slightly. "Of course. I'm a monk. Where else would I be?"

"Then stay there. Don't move. I'm coming to you, and if anyone tries to stop me, they'll regret it. Let's finish this."

Click.

The Buddha calmly set the phone down. One of his men hurried in.

"Master, please — you're in the middle of prayer. We can handle this."

"Buddha, huh? You actually believe that title?" came a voice from the entrance. Bang had arrived faster than expected. "You kidnap children and turn them into assassins. Don't drag Buddha's name into your filth."

The temple guards rushed him. Bang tore through them, his voice cold and tired all at once.

"They took everything from me. My body. My childhood. My family. All to make me into a weapon. And now they want what's left — my life itself."

He kept moving, every strike precise.

"You promised. After every mission, you said I could go free. Live a normal life. I did everything you asked."

The Buddha finally rose from his prayer mat, voice serene. "And I gave you a chance to live. You should be grateful, Bang. All humans die eventually. Death is the one destiny no one escapes. So… farewell."

In his final moments, blood pooling beneath him, Bang whispered one last prayer.

"Buddha… if you're really up there… give me one more chance. Just one. Let me take my revenge on this monster."

---

**Scene shifts one final time.**

Bright lights. The smell of antiseptic. Someone was gently trying to pry his eyes open.

"He's waking up! Officer Kim, can you hear me?"

A woman's voice, anxious and tearful, hovered above him. "Kim Kang-woo! Are you okay? The doctor said you might have memory loss…"

The man on the bed blinked slowly, trying to focus. *Who is Kim Kang-woo?*

The woman noticed his blank expression and her face fell. "Oh no… you really don't remember anything, do you? Here — this is your ID card. And a mirror."

He looked at the ID. Then the mirror.

A young police officer's face stared back at him. Kim Kang-woo. Age twenty-something. Recently injured in the line of duty.

But on the inside, the man behind those eyes was someone else entirely.

*I'm Bang. The legendary assassin. So why am I in this guy's body…?*

And then it hit him. A slow, dangerous smile spread across his new face.

*Buddha actually heard me. I got my second chance.*

*Yuan Gang. You scumbag. I'm coming for you.*
